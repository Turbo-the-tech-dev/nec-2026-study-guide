# NEC 2026 – Article 250: Grounding and Bonding

**⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL FOR EXAMINATION**

---

## Part I – General

### 250.4 – General Requirements for Grounding and Bonding

**250.4(A) – Grounded Systems**
- **(A)(1) – Grounding:** Connect to earth to limit voltage and facilitate overcurrent device operation
- **(A)(2) – Bonding:** Create effective ground-fault current path
- **(A)(3) – Bonding:** Equipotential bonding
- **(A)(4) – Grounding:** Ground non-current-carrying metal parts
- **(A)(5) – Effective Ground-Fault Current Path:** Permanent, low-impedance path

**250.4(B) – Ungrounded Systems**
- Similar requirements for ungrounded systems

---

## Part II – System Grounding

### 250.20 – Alternating-Current Systems to Be Grounded

**Systems Required to Be Grounded:**
- 3-phase, 4-wire wye: All systems
- 3-phase, 4-wire delta: All systems
- Single-phase, 3-wire: All systems
- 50V to 1000V: If can be grounded so max voltage to ground ≤ 150V

### 250.24 – Grounding Service-Supplied AC Systems

**250.24(A) – System Grounding Connections**
- Grounded conductor shall be connected to grounding electrode conductor
- Connection shall be at service disconnect

**250.24(C) – Grounded Conductor Brought to Service Equipment**
- Grounded conductor shall be brought to service equipment

---

## Part III – Grounding Electrode System and Grounding Electrode Conductor

### 250.50 – Grounding Electrode System

All available electrodes shall be bonded together to form grounding electrode system.

### 250.52 – Grounding Electrodes ⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL

**250.52(A) – Electrodes Permitted:**

| Electrode Type | Requirements |
|----------------|--------------|
| **(A)(1) Metal Underground Water Pipe** | In direct contact with earth for 10 ft or more |
| **(A)(2) Metal Frame of Building** | Effectively grounded |
| **(A)(3) Concrete-Encased Electrode** | 20 ft of ½ in rebar OR 20 ft of 4 AWG bare copper |
| **(A)(4) Ground Ring** | 20 ft of 2 AWG bare copper, in direct contact with earth |
| **(A)(5) Rod and Pipe Electrodes** | 8 ft length, 5/8 in rod or ¾ in pipe |
| **(A)(6) Other Listed Electrodes** | Per manufacturer instructions |
| **(A)(7) Plate Electrode** | 2 ft² of surface exposed to soil |

**250.52(B) – Electrodes Not Permitted:**
- Metal underground gas piping
- Aluminum electrodes

### 250.53 – Grounding Electrode System Installation ⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL

**250.53(A)(1) – Rod, Pipe, and Plate Electrodes**
- Lower end shall be at least 8 ft below grade

**250.53(A)(2) – Supplemental Electrode Required**
- If first electrode is rod, pipe, or plate, supplemental electrode required
- **Exception:** If first electrode has resistance ≤ 25 ohms

**250.53(A)(3) – Bonding Jumper**
- Size per 250.66(A)
- Connection per 250.68(A)

**250.53(C) – Rod and Pipe Electrodes**
- Minimum 8 ft in length
- If rock bottom encountered, installed at 45° angle or buried in trench ≥ 2.5 ft deep

**250.53(D) – Plate Electrode**
- Minimum 2 ft² of surface exposed to soil
- Iron or steel: ≥ ¼ in thick
- Nonferrous: ≥ 0.06 in thick

**250.53(E) – Concrete-Encased Electrode**
- 20 ft of ½ in rebar OR 20 ft of 4 AWG bare copper
- Within concrete foundation or footing in direct contact with earth

**250.53(F) – Ground Ring**
- 20 ft of 2 AWG bare copper
- In direct contact with earth
- Buried ≥ 2.5 ft below grade

**250.53(G) – Grounding Electrode Conductor Tap Conductors**
- Tap conductors permitted to connect to common grounding electrode conductor

### 250.56 – Resistance of Rod, Pipe, and Plate Electrodes

**Maximum Resistance:** 25 ohms

**If > 25 ohms:** Supplemental electrode required

### 250.64 – Grounding Electrode Conductor Installation ⭐⭐ HIGH

**250.64(A) – Aluminum or Copper-Clad Aluminum**
- Not permitted in direct contact with earth
- Not permitted within 18 in of earth

**250.64(B) – Securing and Protection**
- If exposed: Protected from physical damage
- If ≤ 6 AWG: In rigid metal conduit, IMC, PVC, or cable armor
- If ≥ 4 AWG: Permitted exposed if not subject to physical damage

**250.64(C) – Continuous**
- Grounding electrode conductor shall be continuous
- **Exceptions:** Splices with irreversible compression connectors or exothermic welding

**250.64(D) – Size of Grounding Electrode Conductor for Alternating-Current Systems**
- Size per 250.66

**250.64(E) – Grounding Electrode Conductor Terminations**
- Terminations shall be accessible
- Exothermic welding or listed connectors

**250.64(F) – Grounding Electrode Conductor Taps**
- Taps permitted in wireways or gutters

### 250.66 – Size of AC Grounding Electrode Conductor ⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL

| Largest Service Conductor | Grounding Electrode Conductor (Copper) | Grounding Electrode Conductor (Aluminum) |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| 2 AWG or smaller | 8 AWG | 6 AWG |
| 1 AWG or 1/0 AWG | 6 AWG | 4 AWG |

| Over 350 kcmil to 600 kcmil | 1/0 AWG | 3/0 AWG |
| Over 600 kcmil | 2/0 AWG | 4/0 AWG |

## Part VI – Equipment Grounding and Equipment Grounding Conductors

### 250.110 – Equipment Fastened in Place or Connected by Permanent Wiring Methods

**Equipment Required to Be Grounded:**
- Switchboards, switchgear, panelboards
- Motor frames
- Enclosures for motor controllers
- Electric signs
- Luminaires
- Receptacles

### 250.112 – Specific Equipment

Additional specific equipment requiring grounding.

### 250.114 – Equipment Connected by Cord and Plug

**Equipment Required to Be Grounded:**
- Hazardous locations
- 120V to 250V, > 1500W
- Handheld motor-operated tools
- Motor-operated appliances
- Cord-and-plug connected equipment in wet locations

### 250.118 – Types of Equipment Grounding Conductors

**Permitted Types:**
- Copper, aluminum, or copper-clad aluminum conductors
- Rigid metal conduit (RMC)
- Intermediate metal conduit (IMC)
- Electrical metallic tubing (EMT)
- Flexible metal conduit (FMC)
- Liquidtight flexible metal conduit (LFMC)
- Metal cable trays
- Cable armor (Type AC)
- Copper sheath of mineral-insulated cable

### 250.120 – Equipment Grounding Conductor Installation

**250.120(A) – Equipment Grounding Conductor Routing**
- With circuit conductors

**250.120(B) – Size**

|-------|------------|
| **Grounding Electrodes (250.52)** | Water pipe, metal frame, concrete-encased, ground ring, rod/pipe, plate |

| **Bonding Jumper Size (250.102)** | Based on largest ungrounded conductor |

### Common Exam Questions:

1. **What is the minimum length for a grounding electrode rod?**
   - Answer: 8 ft per 250.52(A)(5)

2. **When is a supplemental grounding electrode required?**
   - Answer: When first electrode is rod, pipe, or plate per 250.53(A)(2)

3. **What size GEC is required for 4/0 AWG service conductors?**
   - Answer: 2 AWG copper per Table 250.66

4. **What size EGC is required for a 200A breaker?**
   - Answer: 6 AWG copper per Table 250.122

5. **What is the maximum resistance for a rod electrode?**
   - Answer: 25 ohms per 250.56
