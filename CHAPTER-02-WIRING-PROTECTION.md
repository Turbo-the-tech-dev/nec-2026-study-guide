# NEC 2026 – Chapter 2: Wiring and Protection

**Critical for Examination: Articles 210, 215, 230, 240, 250**

---

## Article 200 – Use and Identification of Grounded Conductors

**Purpose:** Establishes requirements for identifying grounded (neutral) conductors.

### Key Requirements:

**200.6 – Means of Identifying Grounded Conductors**
- Size 6 AWG or smaller: Continuous white or gray outer finish, or three continuous white/gray stripes
- Size 4 AWG or larger: White/gray tape, markings, or tagging at terminations

**200.7 – Use of Insulation of a White or Gray Color**
- White/gray insulation shall only be used for grounded conductors
- Exception: Cable assemblies where re-identified for ungrounded use

## Article 210 – Branch Circuits ⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL

**Purpose:** Covers branch-circuit requirements, ratings, and outlet devices.

### Key Sections:

**210.11 – Branch Circuits Required**
- **210.11(C)(1) – Small-Appliance Branch Circuits:** Minimum 2 circuits for kitchen/dining
- **210.11(C)(2) – Laundry Circuit:** Minimum 1 circuit
- **210.11(C)(3) – Bathroom Circuits:** One 20A circuit for all bathroom receptacles

**210.19 – Conductors – Minimum Ampacity**
- **(A)(1) – General:** Branch-circuit conductors shall have ampacity ≥ 125% of continuous load + 100% of noncontinuous load
- **(A)(3) – Small-Appliance Circuits:** Minimum 20A, 12 AWG copper

**210.21 – Outlet Devices**
- **(B)(3) – Receptacle Ratings:**
  | Circuit Rating | Receptacle Rating |
  |----------------|-------------------|
  | 15A | 15A max |
  | 20A | 15A or 20A |
  | 30A | 30A |

**210.52 – Dwelling Unit Receptacle Outlets**
- **(A) – General Provisions:** Every 6 ft along wall line, no point more than 6 ft from receptacle
- **(B) – Small-Appliance Circuits:** Kitchen, pantry, dining room, breakfast room
- **(D) – Bathrooms:** At least one receptacle within 3 ft of basin
- **(G) – Garages and Accessory Buildings:** At least one receptacle

**210.52(D) – Countertops**
- Receptacles at each counter space 12 in or wider
- Spacing: No point more than 24 in horizontally from receptacle

## Article 215 – Feeders ⭐⭐ HIGH

**Purpose:** Covers feeder conductor sizing, overcurrent protection, and identification.

### Key Sections:

**215.2 – Minimum Rating and Size**
- **(A)(1) – Ampacity:** Feeder conductors ≥ 125% of continuous load + 100% of noncontinuous load
- **(A)(3) – Minimum Size:** Feeder conductors shall not be smaller than 8 AWG copper (with exceptions)

**215.12 – Grounded Conductor Identification**
- Same requirements as Article 200

---

## Article 225 – Outside Branch Circuits and Feeders

**Purpose:** Covers requirements for outside branch circuits and feeders.

### Key Sections:

**225.30 – Number of Supplies**
- One supply per building/structure (exceptions apply)

**225.39 – Rating of Disconnect**
- **225.39(A) – One-Circuit Installation:** 15A minimum
- **225.39(B) – Two-Circuit Installation:** 30A minimum
- **225.39(C) – One-Family Dwelling:** 100A minimum
- **225.39(D) – All Others:** 60A minimum

---

## Article 230 – Services ⭐⭐ HIGH

**Purpose:** Covers service conductors, equipment, and disconnecting means.

### Key Sections:

**230.42 – Minimum Ampacity and Size**
- Service conductors ≥ 125% of continuous load + 100% of noncontinuous load

**230.67 – Surge Protection**
- **(A) – Required:** SPD required on all dwelling unit services
- **(B) – Location:** Integral to or adjacent to service equipment

**230.79 – Rating of Service Disconnecting Means**
- **(C) – One-Family Dwelling:** 100A minimum

**230.85 – Emergency Disconnects**
- Service equipment for dwellings shall have emergency disconnect

---

## Article 240 – Overcurrent Protection ⭐⭐ HIGH

**Purpose:** Covers overcurrent protection of conductors and equipment.

### Key Sections:

**240.4 – Protection of Conductors**
- Conductors shall be protected against overcurrent

**240.6 – Standard Ampere Ratings**
- 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 60, 70, 80, 90, 100, 110, 125, 150, 175, 200, 225, 250, 300, 350, 400, 450, 500, 600, 700, 800, 1000, 1200, 1600, 2000, 2500, 3000, 4000, 5000, 6000A

**240.21 – Location in Circuit**
- Overcurrent device shall be at point where conductor receives supply

**240.24 – Location in or on Premises**
- Readily accessible location

## Article 250 – Grounding and Bonding ⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL

**Purpose:** Covers grounding and bonding requirements for electrical systems.

### Key Sections:

**250.24 – Grounding Service-Supplied Alternating-Current Systems**
- **(A) – System Grounding Connections:** Grounded conductor shall be connected to grounding electrode

**250.30 – Grounding Separately Derived Alternating-Current Systems**
- Requirements for grounding separately derived systems

**250.52 – Grounding Electrodes**
- **(A)(1) – Metal Underground Water Pipe:** In direct contact with earth for 10 ft or more
- **(A)(2) – Metal Frame of Building:** Effectively grounded
- **(A)(3) – Concrete-Encased Electrode:** 20 ft of ½ in rebar or bare copper conductor
- **(A)(4) – Ground Ring:** 20 ft of bare copper, 2 AWG or larger, in direct contact with earth
- **(A)(5) – Rod and Pipe Electrodes:** 8 ft in length, 5/8 in rod or ¾ in pipe
- **(A)(7) – Plate Electrode:** 2 ft² of surface exposed to soil

**250.53 – Grounding Electrode System Installation**
- **(A)(2) – Supplemental Electrode Required:** If first electrode is rod/pipe/plate, supplemental required
- **(A)(3) – Bonding Jumper:** 6 AWG copper minimum

**250.64 – Grounding Electrode Conductor Installation**
- **(A) – Aluminum or Copper-Clad Aluminum:** Not permitted in direct contact with earth
- **(B) – Securing and Protection:** If exposed, shall be protected from physical damage

**250.66 – Size of Alternating-Current Grounding Electrode Conductor**

| Largest Service Conductor | Grounding Electrode Conductor |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 2 AWG or smaller | 8 AWG copper |
| 1 AWG or 1/0 AWG | 6 AWG copper |
| 2/0 AWG or 3/0 AWG | 4 AWG copper |
| Over 3/0 AWG to 350 kcmil | 2 AWG copper |
| Over 350 kcmil to 600 kcmil | 1/0 AWG copper |
| Over 600 kcmil | 2/0 AWG copper |

**250.102 – Bonding Conductors and Equipment**
- Requirements for bonding conductors

**250.122 – Size of Equipment Grounding Conductors**

| Overcurrent Device Rating | EGC Size (Copper) |
|---------------------------|-------------------|
| 15A | 14 AWG |
| 20A | 12 AWG |
| 30A | 10 AWG |
| 40A | 10 AWG |
| 50A | 10 AWG |
| 60A | 10 AWG |
| 100A | 8 AWG |
| 200A | 6 AWG |
| 400A | 3 AWG |
| 600A | 1 AWG |

### Common Exam Questions:

1. **What is the minimum number of small-appliance branch circuits for a kitchen?**
   - Answer: 2 per 210.11(C)(1)

2. **What is the minimum service rating for a one-family dwelling?**
   - Answer: 100A per 230.79(C)

3. **What size equipment grounding conductor is required for a 200A service?**
   - Answer: 6 AWG copper per Table 250.122

4. **How far apart must receptacles be placed in a dwelling?**
   - Answer: No point more than 6 ft from a receptacle per 210.52(A)

5. **When is a supplemental grounding electrode required?**
   - Answer: When the first electrode is a rod, pipe, or plate per 250.53(A)(2)
